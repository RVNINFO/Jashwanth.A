The Amazon Rainforest, spanning over 5.5 million square kilometers, plays a crucial role in regulating the Earth's climate. Often referred to as the "lungs of the Earth", it absorbs vast amounts of carbon dioxide and produces 20% of our planet's oxygen. Recent satellite data, however, reveals that deforestation has surged by 22% in the past year alone. Illegal logging and mining continue to threaten biodiversity. The Ganges River, one of the holiest and most essential rivers in India, provides water to over 400 million people. A recent environmental survey highlighted increased levels of industrial pollutants and untreated sewage. Despite several clean-up initiatives like "Namami Gange", experts warn that urbanization without adequate infrastructure continues to degrade the river's health. Citizens and NGOs are pushing for stricter enforcement of water treatment laws.

The Himalayan mountain range, home to Mount Everest and over 50 other peaks above 7,000 meters, is undergoing rapid change. Glaciologists report that glaciers are melting at double the rate compared to the previous century, driven by climate change. This directly affects water supply to nations downstream, including India, Nepal, Bhutan, and China. The loss of glacial mass also increases the risk of flash floods and landslides, especially during monsoon season.
